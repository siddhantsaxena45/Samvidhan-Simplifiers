Article 5 of the Indian Constitution deals with the citizenship of persons who were in India at the commencement of the Constitution. It stipulates that those who were Indian citizens at the time of the Constitution's commencement would continue to be citizens.

Article 6 addresses the citizenship of persons who migrated from Pakistan to India. It provides for the conditions under which such individuals and their descendants would be recognized as Indian citizens. Specifically, it details the criteria for citizenship based on residency and registration for those who migrated before or after the Constitution's commencement, ensuring their rights and status as Indian citizens.
